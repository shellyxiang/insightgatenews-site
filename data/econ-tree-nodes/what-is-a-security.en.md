## What Is a Security?

A security is a standardized and tradable financial claim. It gives its holder some recognized claim on future payments, profits, or assets, and it is designed to be transferable rather than tied permanently to the original issuer-holder relationship. This is what makes securities so important in modern finance: they allow financial claims to circulate through markets.

The idea may sound technical, but it is actually quite simple. If one person lends directly to another, the relationship stays personal and specific. A security changes that. It turns the claim into a more general instrument that can be held by many investors, priced by the market, and traded after it is issued. This makes finance much more flexible and much larger in scale.

Securities can represent different kinds of claims. Some promise repayment with interest. Others represent ownership and a share in future profits. What they have in common is that they transform financial rights into forms that can move through markets. In this way, securities help connect issuers, investors, and the broader financial system.
