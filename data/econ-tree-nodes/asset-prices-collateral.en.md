## Asset Prices and Collateral

Asset prices and collateral play a central role in the credit cycle because rising asset values can support more borrowing. If property prices, bond prices, or other assets increase, borrowers may appear stronger, lenders may feel safer, and the same assets may support larger loans. In this way, finance does not simply respond to asset prices. It often reinforces them.

This dynamic is especially important when lending depends on collateral. A house, a financial portfolio, or some other asset may be used to secure borrowing. If the value of that collateral rises, lenders may be willing to extend more credit on better terms. This can create a feedback loop in which rising prices support more borrowing, and more borrowing helps push prices higher.

The problem is that this process also works in reverse. If asset prices begin to fall, collateral values weaken, borrowing capacity shrinks, and lenders become more cautious. This can intensify the downturn. For this reason, asset prices and collateral are among the most important amplifiers in the credit cycle. They connect market valuations to the ability of borrowers to keep financing themselves.
