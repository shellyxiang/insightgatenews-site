## Obligation and Deferred Payment

The starting point of credit is not the modern bank loan. It is the much simpler idea that payment does not have to happen immediately. One person can receive something now and promise to repay later. This creates an obligation that stretches across time. In that moment, exchange is no longer purely immediate. It becomes a relationship between present value and future repayment.

This matters because many economic activities do not fit neatly into instant exchange. A farmer may need seeds before harvest. A merchant may need goods before sale. A household may need support before income arrives. Deferred payment allows economic life to continue even when resources and needs do not line up perfectly at the same moment.

Historically, this was one of the earliest financial innovations, even if people did not think of it in modern financial language. Once societies accepted that obligations could be carried into the future, they opened the door to debt, credit, accounting, interest, and eventually formal lending systems. Deferred payment is therefore one of the clearest points at which economic life begins to take on a financial dimension.
