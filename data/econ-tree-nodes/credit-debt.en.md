## Credit & Debt

Credit and debt are among the oldest foundations of economic order. Long before modern banks, people were already making promises, recording obligations, and settling claims over time. In this sense, finance did not begin with stock markets or central banks. It began much earlier, when exchange no longer happened only on the spot and economic life started to depend on trust, memory, and enforceable obligation.

This branch explains how societies moved from immediate exchange to systems in which payment could be delayed, claims could be recorded, and relationships could extend across time. Once this happened, economic life became more flexible but also more dependent on trust, record-keeping, and authority. Credit and debt made it possible to bridge gaps between production and payment, need and supply, present resources and future income. They were therefore not a late addition to economic life, but a central part of how complex economies became possible.
