# Cyprus: Offshore-Style Services, EU Framework, and a Regional Business Hub

Cyprus is best mapped as a **services-led island economy** with a strong **international business and financial-services footprint** under an EU legal framework. Its structural identity is shaped by (1) cross-border corporate and professional services, (2) tourism and maritime-related services, and (3) the legacy of a major banking crisis and subsequent regulatory tightening.

## Key facts & structural features

- **Service-based economy:** Cyprus relies heavily on services (professional/business services, tourism, shipping-related activity) rather than manufacturing.
- **Banking crisis legacy:** Cyprus experienced a major banking crisis in 2012–2013, followed by restructuring and stronger regulation—this shapes its modern financial identity.
- **EU member & euro area:** Cyprus is an EU member and uses the euro, giving it euro-area monetary conditions and legal/contract stability.

## Core Pillars (Financial Layer)

**International Business Services + Euro-Area Legal Stability**
A services-export economy leveraging EU/euro-area frameworks for cross-border corporate and professional services.

## Global Identity (Intuitive Layer)

**Mediterranean Business & Services Island**
Known for international business services, tourism, and regional connectivity.
