# Bhutan — Quick Facts


- **Regional logic:**a **small Himalayan hydropower economy**, not as part of East Asia.
- **Basic economic profile:** A **small highland economy** with hydropower, public spending, tourism, and state-guided development.
- **Main economic base:** Hydropower exports are the clearest pillar, while tourism and public investment also matter.
- **Key structural feature:** Bhutan combines **small scale, strong state direction, and reliance on hydropower** more clearly than most other South Asian economies.
- **Main current issues:** Growth is recovering strongly, but debt management, small market size, and reliance on a few sectors remain important constraints.
- **Why it matters in the map:** It helps show that South Asia includes **small highland states** whose economic logic is very different from the region’s large population economies.
- **Best short reading:** A small Himalayan economy built on hydropower, state-guided development, and limited but high-value tourism.
