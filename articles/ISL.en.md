# Iceland: Geothermal Energy and a Post-Crisis Small Open Economy

Iceland is best mapped as a **small open economy** with a distinctive structural pillar: **geothermal-based energy** (plus hydropower), which supports energy-intensive activity and high energy security. Iceland also carries a defining macro-financial memory: a major banking collapse in 2008, after which it rebuilt its financial system and policy framework.

## Key facts & structural features

- **Geothermal leadership:** Iceland is globally known for large-scale geothermal utilization for electricity and heating.
- **2008 banking collapse:** Iceland's 2008 financial crisis was a defining turning point shaping its modern macro-financial identity.
- **Small open economy logic:** external demand (tourism, exports) and financial conditions matter disproportionately for growth and stability.

## Core Pillars (Financial Layer)

**Geothermal Energy Base + Post-Crisis Macro Discipline**
A small open economy with strong domestic energy foundations and a financial system shaped by the post-2008 restructuring.

## Global Identity (Intuitive Layer)

Known for geothermal energy and a post-crisis small-state macro narrative.
