# Georgia: Black Sea Corridor Economy with Remittance & Services Inflows

Georgia's core structural identity is a **corridor economy** (Black Sea–Caucasus link) combined with an **open external-balance model** supported by services and inflows.

## Key facts & structural features

- **Corridor role:** Georgia sits on major East–West routes; the **Baku–Tbilisi–Ceyhan (BTC) pipeline** runs from Azerbaijan to Turkey via Georgia.
- **Open-economy financing:** The IMF expects Georgia's current account deficit to widen as remittances normalize.
- **Remittances are material:** World Bank data shows **remittances ≈ 11.9% of GDP (2024)**.
- **Services exports matter:** Transport and IT services play an important role in the external balance dynamics.

## Core Pillars (Financial Layer)

**Black Sea Corridor + Inflow-Supported External Balance**
A transit-and-services economy where remittances and services help finance external needs.

## Global Identity (Intuitive Layer)

**Caucasus Gateway (Black Sea Connector)**
Known as a strategic corridor linking the Caspian region to wider markets.
