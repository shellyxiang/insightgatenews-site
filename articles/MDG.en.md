# Madagascar — Quick Facts

- **Regional placement:** Southern Africa for this project, mainly because Madagascar is a **SADC member**, even though it is an island in the Indian Ocean.
- **Basic economic profile:** A **large island economy** with agriculture, mining, textiles, and tourism all playing visible roles.
- **Main economic base:** Agriculture still matters deeply for livelihoods, while exports include products such as vanilla, cloves, nickel, cobalt, and textiles.
- **Growth pattern:** The economy has been recovering, but growth remains **uneven and not strong enough to sharply improve living standards**.
- **Main structural constraints:** Low productivity, infrastructure weakness, climate vulnerability, and high poverty.
- **Why it matters regionally:** Madagascar is not a mainland corridor economy like Mozambique, but it matters as a **large Indian Ocean island economy linked to Southern Africa through SADC**.
- **Best short reading:** A broad but still low-productivity island economy with agriculture at the base and mining/textiles/tourism adding external weight.
