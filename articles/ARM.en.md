# Armenia: Diaspora-Linked Services Economy under Corridor Constraints

Armenia's structural identity is **not energy-export-led**; it is better described as a **diaspora-linked, services-and-capability economy** operating with tighter geographic/corridor constraints.

## Key facts & structural features

- **Remittances matter:** World Bank data shows **remittances ≈ 4.9% of GDP (2024)**.
- **Services + remittances channel:** IMF analysis treats remittances and service exports as key macro channels in Armenia's outlook.
- **Small open "catch-up" profile:** External conditions and partner growth matter disproportionately for a small economy.
- **Policy capacity focus:** Macro stability is framed around maintaining buffers and managing shocks rather than global financial influence.

## Core Pillars (Financial Layer)

**Diaspora Remittances + Services/IT-Style Tradables**
A capability-building model where inflows and services exports support demand and external stability.

## Global Identity (Intuitive Layer)

**Diaspora-Linked Caucasus Economy**
Known for strong diaspora connections shaping consumption and external financing.
