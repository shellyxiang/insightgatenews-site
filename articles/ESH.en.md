# Western Sahara — Quick Facts

- **Project placement:** Special political-economic case, not a normal sovereign-country entry.
- **Political status:** In African Union materials, it appears as the **Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic (SADR)**, an AU member since 1982. In wider international politics, Western Sahara remains a disputed territory question.
- **Basic economic profile:** A **small, externally disputed desert economy** with very limited broad domestic structure.
- **Main economic base:** The territory is most commonly associated with **phosphates and fishing**, rather than with a diversified domestic economy.
- **Key structural feature:** Its economy is inseparable from the political dispute over sovereignty and territorial control.
- **Why it matters in the map:** It is useful as a **special disputed-resource case**, not as a standard national economy.
- **Best short reading:** A disputed North-West African territory whose economic relevance comes mainly from phosphates, fisheries, and political status rather than from a broad national economic structure.
