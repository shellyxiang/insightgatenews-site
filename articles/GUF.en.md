# French Guiana — Quick Facts

- **Project placement:** North South America supplement / French overseas economy.
- **Regional logic:** French Guiana is geographically in **South America**, but politically it is an **overseas department and region of France** and therefore part of the French Republic and the European Union.
- **Basic economic profile:** A **small, state-supported service economy** with a narrow private sector and strong dependence on public spending.
- **Main economic base:** The economy relies heavily on the **public sector**, transfers linked to France, the **Guiana Space Centre** in Kourou, construction, and local services.
- **Key structural feature:** French Guiana is not a typical independent South American commodity economy. It is a **South American territory whose economy is strongly shaped by French state support and European institutional links**.
- **Secondary sectors:** Fisheries, some gold-related activity, and limited local production matter, but they do not define the economy in the way oil defines Guyana or mining defines Suriname.
- **Why it stands out:** Its economy is unusually influenced by **space activity, public administration, and external fiscal support**, rather than by a broad domestic market or diversified export base.
- **Best short reading:** A French-supported South American territorial economy built on public services, the space center, and limited local production.
