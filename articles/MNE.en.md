# Montenegro: Euroized Micro-Economy Driven by Adriatic Tourism

Montenegro is a small Adriatic country in the Western Balkans whose economic identity is unusually clear: **tourism dominates**, and the country **uses the euro de facto** despite not being an EU or euro-area member. Structurally, Montenegro behaves like a tourism-export micro-economy with limited policy tools and high sensitivity to external demand and seasonal cycles.

## Key facts and structural features

- **Tourism-led economy:** Tourism is described as the most important sector, accounting for **around 30% of GDP**, with **2.61 million visitors** and **15.59 million overnight stays** in 2024 (96% foreign stays). 
- **Euro de facto currency:** Montenegro uses the **euro as a de facto domestic currency** without a formal agreement with the EU. 
- **Small-country specialization:** The model relies on coastal services, hospitality investment, and foreign tourism inflows—more than on large-scale manufacturing or deep capital markets. 
- **EU path (still outside):** Montenegro is an EU candidate; accession negotiations started in **June 2012**.
