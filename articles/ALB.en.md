# Albania: Tourism Surge, Hydropower Dependence, and Lek-Based Catch-Up Growth

Albania is a small Western Balkan economy with a very clear recent trajectory: **fast tourism expansion**, a power system dominated by **hydropower**, and a **bank-centered financial system** operating under the **Albanian lek (ALL)** (not the euro). Structurally, Albania behaves like an EU-adjacent "catch-up" economy where external inflows (tourism receipts and remittances) matter for demand and foreign-currency liquidity.

## Key facts and structural features

- **EU path (institutional convergence):** Albania became an EU candidate in **June 2014** and accession negotiations began in **July 2022**.
- **Currency:** The official currency is the **Albanian lek (ALL)**.
- **Tourism-led external income:** Albania recorded about **11.7 million foreign visitors in 2024**, according to the tourism ministry as reported by Euronews Albania.
- **Remittances remain important:** World Bank-based estimates put remittances at **~8.37% of GDP in 2024**.
- **Hydropower-heavy electricity system:** Renewable power capacity is predominantly hydropower—about **~95% of generating capacity**, per UNECE.

## Core Pillars (Financial Layer)

**Tourism & Remittance Inflows + Hydropower-Based Energy System**
External inflows support consumption and FX liquidity, while electricity supply is structurally exposed to hydrology (water availability).

## Global Identity (Intuitive Layer)

**"New" Adriatic Tourism Destination**
A rapidly rising Mediterranean-adjacent tourism market in the Western Balkans, operating with its own currency under EU-candidate convergence.
