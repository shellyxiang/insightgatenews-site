# Equatorial Guinea — Quick Facts

- **Regional placement:** Central Africa.
- **Regional logic:** Best treated as a **Central African oil-state supplement**, alongside Gabon and Congo.
- **Basic economic profile:** A **small, hydrocarbon-heavy economy** with very high export concentration.
- **Main economic base:** Oil and gas remain the dominant pillars, though the country is trying to diversify.
- **Key structural feature:** The country has had significant resource wealth, but the domestic economy remains much thinner than the export story suggests.
- **Main current issues:** Declining hydrocarbon production, weak medium-term growth, and the challenge of building non-oil sectors.
- **Why it matters regionally:** It is important as a **small but extreme example of a hydrocarbon-centered Central African economy**.
- **Best short reading:** A small oil-and-gas state with high resource concentration, limited diversification, and a domestic base weaker than its export earnings imply.
