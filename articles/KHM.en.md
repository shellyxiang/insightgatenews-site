# Cambodia: Garments, Construction, and a Small Open Economy Facing Slower Momentum

Cambodia is a **small Southeast Asian economy whose structure is shaped by garments, tourism, construction, and an open trade model that remains vulnerable to external demand and domestic financial weakness**. It is not a large manufacturing economy in the East Asian sense, and it is not a broad domestic-market economy. Its economic role comes from a different combination: labor-intensive exports, tourism-linked services, construction activity, and an economic model that has grown quickly but remains narrow and exposed.

## 1. Garments Remain the Core Export Pillar

Cambodia’s clearest external economic pillar remains garments and related light manufacturing. Export-oriented production has been central to growth, employment, and integration into global markets. This gives Cambodia one of the clearest labor-intensive export profiles in Southeast Asia.

## 2. Tourism and Services Matter Alongside Manufacturing

Tourism has long been one of Cambodia’s most visible economic sectors, and service activity remains important to jobs and external earnings. This makes Cambodia more than just a garment economy, even though manufacturing remains central. The country’s structure is best understood as a mix of light industry and service dependence.

## 3. Construction and Property Have Played an Outsized Role

A key feature of Cambodia’s economic rise has been the importance of construction and real estate. These sectors helped fuel growth for years, but they also contributed to financial vulnerabilities. This means Cambodia’s development model has not only been export-dependent, but also partly tied to a domestically fragile property cycle.

## 4. Recent Growth Has Slowed Because the Model Is Narrow

Cambodia can still grow, but the economy has become more vulnerable to changes in tourism, remittances, external demand, and financial conditions. Slower momentum does not mean the model has disappeared. It means that a narrow growth structure is more exposed when external support weakens.

## 5. The Main Structural Question Is Whether Cambodia Can Broaden Beyond a Narrow Export-and-Construction Model

The deeper issue for Cambodia is not whether garments and tourism can continue to support growth. They can. The more important question is whether the country can build stronger productivity, more diversified industry, and a more stable domestic economic base. In other words, the challenge is not only growth, but widening the structure behind it.

## Conclusion

In the global system, Cambodia is best read as a **small open Southeast Asian economy built on garments, tourism, and construction, but still constrained by narrow diversification and financial vulnerability**. Its distinct role comes from being one of the region’s clearest labor-intensive export economies without yet developing a broad and resilient national base.
