# Guinea-Bissau — Quick Facts

- **Regional placement:** West Africa.
- **Regional logic:** Best treated as a **West African supplement**, not as an East, Central, or Southern African case.
- **Basic economic profile:** A **small, low-income, agriculture-heavy economy** with very limited diversification.
- **Main economic base:** Cashew nuts dominate exports, while agriculture employs a large share of the labor force.
- **Key structural feature:** Guinea-Bissau is one of the clearest cases of a country whose economy is highly exposed to **one crop, political uncertainty, and weak institutional capacity**.
- **Main current issues:** Heavy dependence on cashew, weak diversification, poverty, and exposure to domestic political shocks.
- **Why it matters regionally:** It is useful in the map as a **small fragile West African cashew-export economy**, not because of scale but because of how concentrated its structure is.
- **Best short reading:** A small West African agriculture-export economy built heavily around cashew, with growth potential but very shallow diversification.
