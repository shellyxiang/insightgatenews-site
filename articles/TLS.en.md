# Timor-Leste — Quick Facts


- **Regional logic:** Timor-Leste now fits more naturally into the Southeast Asia map because it was formally admitted as **ASEAN’s 11th member in October 2025**.
- **Basic economic profile:** A **small post-conflict economy** with a narrow non-oil base and very high reliance on public spending supported by petroleum wealth.
- **Main economic base:** The economy still depends heavily on the **Petroleum Fund**, while agriculture and small-scale domestic activity remain important for livelihoods.
- **Key structural feature:** Timor-Leste is not a diversified manufacturing economy; it is a **small state trying to turn oil wealth into long-term development capacity**.
- **Main current issues:** The non-oil economy remains weak, fiscal and external imbalances are large, and long-term sustainability depends on building stronger non-oil sectors.
- **Why it matters in the map:** It helps show that Southeast Asia includes not only export-manufacturing and service hubs, but also **small post-conflict states supported by resource funds**.
- **Best short reading:** A small Southeast Asian economy supported by petroleum wealth, still searching for a viable non-oil future.
