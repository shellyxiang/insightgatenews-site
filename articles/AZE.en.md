# Azerbaijan: Oil & Gas Export State with Sovereign Wealth Buffer

Azerbaijan's structural identity is a **hydrocarbon-export state**, where fiscal capacity, external balances, and reserves are heavily shaped by oil and gas revenues and managed via large state buffers.

## Key facts & structural features

- **Hydrocarbon dependence:** The economy is heavily dependent on oil and gas for exports and budget revenues.
- **Sovereign wealth buffer (SOFAZ):** Azerbaijan's **State Oil Fund (SOFAZ)** serves as a key macro-financial stabilizer.
- **Large combined reserves:** IMF reporting notes combined reserves of the central bank and SOFAZ reaching very large levels, providing a significant macro buffer.
- **Energy corridor infrastructure:** The BTC pipeline is a flagship export corridor connecting Azerbaijan to Turkey via Georgia.

## Core Pillars (Financial Layer)

**Oil & Gas Revenues + Sovereign Wealth Stabilization**
Hydrocarbon rents fund the state and build buffers that stabilize macro-finance.

## Global Identity (Intuitive Layer)

**Caspian Energy Export State**
Known for oil/gas exports and strategic pipeline corridors.
