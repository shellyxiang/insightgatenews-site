# Fiji: Tourism, Services, and the Core Island Economy of the Pacific

Fiji is a **Pacific island economy whose structure is shaped by tourism, services, remittances, and a stronger domestic and regional role than most other island states in Oceania**. It is not a resource-heavy economy, and it is not a high-income advanced state. Its economic role comes from a different combination: tourism, transportation and services, some agriculture, and its position as one of the Pacific’s most important island hubs.

## 1. Fiji Is the Pacific’s Most Important Island Economy

Fiji stands out in the Pacific because it is both one of the most populous and one of the most economically significant island states. This gives it a role broader than that of many smaller Pacific economies. It is not just another tourism island. It is one of the Pacific’s core service and connectivity economies.

## 2. Tourism Remains the Main Growth Driver

Tourism is the clearest pillar of Fiji’s economy. Visitor flows support jobs, external earnings, and domestic demand. This makes Fiji one of the most tourism-dependent economies in Oceania outside the advanced economies.

## 3. Services and Regional Connectivity Add Breadth

Fiji’s economy is broader than tourism alone. Transport, commerce, public services, and regional business activity all matter. This gives Fiji a stronger service base and more regional relevance than many other Pacific island economies.

## 4. Recovery Continued, but Growth Is Slowing

Recent IMF and World Bank materials note that Fiji’s post-pandemic recovery continued into 2024, but that growth was slowing in 2025 as tourism momentum moderated. This means the economy remains on a recovery path, but the most rapid rebound phase has already passed.

## 5. The Main Structural Question Is Whether Fiji Can Move from Recovery to Higher Productivity

The deeper issue for Fiji is not whether tourism can keep supporting the economy. It can. The more important question is whether a tourism- and services-led island economy can strengthen productivity, fiscal buffers, and resilience against future shocks. In other words, the challenge is not only recovery, but quality and resilience after recovery.

## Conclusion

In the global system, Fiji is best read as a **tourism-and-services Pacific island economy built on external demand, regional hub functions, and a relatively broader service base than most island peers**. Its distinct role comes from being the Pacific’s clearest island-service center outside Australia and New Zealand.
