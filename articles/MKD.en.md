# North Macedonia: EU-Candidate Manufacturing Node with Remittance Support

North Macedonia is a small Western Balkan economy whose structure is shaped by EU-candidate institutional convergence, FDI-linked light manufacturing/assembly exports, and meaningful diaspora remittances. It is not a financial hub; finance is largely bank-centered, while macro stability is managed under a domestic currency (the Macedonian denar, MKD) rather than the euro.

## Key facts and structural features

- **EU path:** EU candidate since 2005; accession negotiations began in 2022.
- **Currency:** Macedonian denar (MKD), not euroized.
- **Remittances:** an important external income channel (tracked by the World Bank as % of GDP).
- **Economic base:** industry and services mix; exports and investment often tied to EU-adjacent supply chains rather than domestic scale.

## Core Pillars (Financial Layer)

**FDI-Linked Export Manufacturing + Denar-Based Macro Finance**
A small open economy upgrading through EU-linked production and investment, operating under domestic-currency macro management.

## Global Identity (Intuitive Layer)

**Western Balkans EU-Candidate Manufacturing Node**
Known as an EU-adjacent production and services economy, supported by diaspora links and ongoing institutional convergence.
